Top 5

  1. Call Me by Your Name (2017)

    Genre:Romantic Drama

    Director:Luca Guadagnino

    Screenplay:James Ivory (based on the novel by André Aciman)

    Short summary: Set in Northern Italy in the summer of 1983, the film follows 17-year-old Elio who is spending his vacation at his family’s villa.
    When Oliver, a charming American student, comes to stay as an intern for Elio's father,
    a close bond forms between the two. As the summer progresses, their relationship deepens into a passionate and emotional connection.
    The film beautifully portrays the experience of first love, longing, and self-discovery.

    Quote:

    "I'm like you", he said."I remember everything".
    I stopped for a second. If you remember everything, I wanted to say, and if you are really like me,
    then before you leave tomorrow, or when you’re just ready to shut the door of the taxi and have already said goodbye to everyone else
    and there’s not a thing left to say in this life, then, just this once, turn to me, even in jest, or as an afterthought,
    which would have meant everything to me when we were together, and, as you did back then, look me in the face, hold my gaze,
    and call me by your name”

  1. Jojo Rabbit (2019)

    Genre:Comedy, Drama, War

    Director & Screenwriter:Taika Waititi (based on the novel Caging Skies by Christine Leunens)

    Short summary: Jojo Rabbit is a satirical World War II film that follows Jojo, a lonely 10-year-old German boy who is a devoted member of the Hitler Youth.
    His world turns upside down when he discovers that his mother is hiding a Jewish girl in their attic.
    Struggling with his blind nationalism, Jojo turns to his imaginary friend — a comically absurd version of Adolf Hitler — for guidance.
    As Jojo begins to learn more about Elsa and question what he’s been taught, he undergoes a journey of transformation, empathy, and love.

    Quote:

    Enjoy the attention, kid. Not everyone’s lucky enough to look stupid. I for one am cursed to look incredibly attractive.
    Now, we’re going to pluck up your courage, walk out that door, and have an incredible adventure. Okay?


  1. Eternal Sunshine of The Spotless Mind (2004)

    Genre:Science Fiction, Romance, Drama

    Director:Michel Gondry

    Screenplay:Charlie Kaufman (story by Kaufman, Gondry, and Pierre Bismuth)

    Short summary: The film tells the story of Joel and Clementine, a couple who, after a painful breakup, undergo a medical procedure to erase all memories of each other.
    As Joel relives their shared moments during the memory-erasing process, he begins to realize that he doesn't want to forget.
    The film creatively explores memory, love, regret, and the human desire to hold on to meaningful experiences — even painful ones.

    Quote:

    “We met at the wrong time. That’s what I keep telling myself anyway.
    Maybe one day years from now, we’ll meet in a coffee shop in a far away city somewhere and we could give it another shot.”

  1. I Origins (2014)

    Genre:Science Fiction, Drama, Romance

    Director & Writer:Mike Cahill

    Short summary: I Origins follows Ian Gray, a molecular biologist studying the evolution of the human eye.
    After meeting a mysterious woman with unique eyes, his life takes an unexpected turn.
    Following a tragic event, Ian makes a scientific discovery that challenges his beliefs about life, death, and the possibility of reincarnation.
    The film explores the intersection of science and spirituality,questioning whether there are things science cannot yet explain.

    Quote:

    “Ian: I don't believe in luck. I do believe we've known each other since forever, though. Sofi: Really?
    Ian: Yeah. You know how? When the big bang happened, all the atoms in the universe,
    they were all smashed together into one little dot that exploded outward.
    So my atoms and your atoms were certainly together then, and, who knows,
    probably smashed together several times in the last 13.7 billion years.
    So my atoms have known your atoms and they've always known your atoms.
    My atoms have always loved your atoms.”

  1. Little Miss Sunshine (2006)

    Genre:Comedy, Drama, Road Movie

    Directors:Jonathan Dayton & Valerie Faris

    Writer:Michael Arndt

    Short summary: Little Miss Sunshine follows the dysfunctional Hoover family as they embark on a cross-country road trip in their old VW van to get young Olive to a children’s beauty pageant.
    Along the way, each family member confronts personal struggles and failures, but their shared journey brings them closer together.
    The film balances humor with heartfelt moments, exploring themes of family, failure, and resilience.

    Quote:

    Olive: Grandpa, am I pretty?
    Grandpa: Olive, you are the most beautiful girl in the whole world.
    Olive: Nah, you're just saying that.
    Grandpa: No, I'm not. I'm madly in love with you, and it's not because of your brains or your personality.
    It's because you're beautiful, inside and out.
